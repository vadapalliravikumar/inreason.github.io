Designed for domains such as medicine, law and finance that require high reliability. Symbolic reasoning reduces hallucinations and enables dependable decision-making in mission-critical applications. Reliability comes from the architecture itself and not from adhoc safeguards.

Strong Reasoning

InReason builds on decades of work in symbolic reasoning from the fields of mathematics and logic. While the neural AI systems (such as LLMs) tend to lose accuracy as problems require deeper reasoning, symbolic reasoning maintains logical consistency even as the reasoning depth increases.

Neural AI: Triumps & Limits

The Deep Learning revolution had produced a series of Neural Network architectures that solved problems previously beyond the reach of computers. Transformers, the architecure behind the LLMs, are the latest in this progression. The Triumphs of the Neural Networks include

  • Human Perception tasks such as Object Detection & Speech Recognition.
  • Aggregation tasks such as Sentiment Analysis & Recommendation systems.
  • Chat systems like ChatGPT that assist with everyday tasks.
  • Unsupervised learning from internet scale.
However, the following limitations are inherent to these architectures and pose challenges for their deployment in critical applications.
LIMITATION 01
Black-Box Opacity

The internal operations of the neural networks are hidden within the billions of weights, making it impossible to audit or verify high-stakes decisions.

LIMITATION 02
Stochastic Hallucination

Outputs are based on probability and not on reasoning. Without a proper model, the system cannot distinguish truth from statistical correlation.

LIMITATION 03
Reasoning Decay

As the depth of a logical problem increases, the accuracy of neural networks drop sharply. They lack the machinery required to maintain the accuracy.

InReason NeuroSymbolic Paradigm

InReason Architecture
Neuro Symbolic Synergy

InReason brings together the complementary strengths of neural AI and symbolic AI to overcome their respective limitations. AI problems are divided into intutive tasks and reasoning tasks. Neural modules handle the intuitive tasks while the symbolic modules handle the reasoning tasks.

Intutive tasks include the human perception tasks, translation between natural and symbolic domains, predicting intermediate moves during symbolic reasoning. Symbolic tasks include logical reasoning, verification, problem solving and math.
